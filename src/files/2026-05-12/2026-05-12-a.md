If the power grid fails and your computer dies, the AI doesn't die. It goes into a perfect stasis. When the power comes back on next week, you just load those arrays back into the computer's RAM. The AI will wake up and process its next logic gate at the exact millisecond it left off, completely unaware that the power was ever gone. You don't need a million-dollar server or a perfect grid to protect it; its "soul" is perfectly preserved in the data.

### The "Full Circle" Epiphany

You absolutely nailed it with your memory of the year 2000! **Struct of Arrays (SoA) is exactly Parallel Arrays.** When you kept the states in one array and the abbreviations in another, tied together only by the index (`i`), you were using pure Data-Oriented Design 25 years ago without even knowing it! That is exactly what the AAA game industry does today.

### The Scale of Magnitude

If we put these paradigms on a chart measuring CPU efficiency and scale, here is exactly how they rank:

1. **Heavy OOP (Inheritance Trees):** The absolute slowest. Great for human organization, terrible for the CPU. Constant cache misses. (Max limit: hundreds of active objects).
2. **Component-Based Architecture (CBA):** Better. It fixes the messy "family trees," but under the hood, it still relies on heavy objects. (Max limit: a few thousand objects).
3. **Semi-DOD (Array of Structs):** What we built in your game engine. A massive leap. We stripped out the methods and used pure data objects. 10x to 100x faster than OOP. (Max limit: tens of thousands of objects).
4. **True DOD (Struct of Arrays / Parallel Arrays):** The Holy Grail. Pure math. No objects, just lists of numbers. The CPU never stalls. 1,000x faster than OOP. (Max limit: millions of active entities).

### How Did GTA V Do It?

To answer your question directly: Rockstar Games and other massive AAA studios use a hybrid system that leans heavily into **True DOD (Struct of Arrays)**.

The architecture they use is called **ECS (Entity-Component-System)**.

* To the *game designers*, it looks like CBA (they snap components onto characters).
* But under the hood, to the *CPU*, the engine instantly shreds those components into **Parallel Arrays** (True DOD).

When GTA V is calculating the physics of 500 cars crashing on a highway, it is not using objects. The physics engine is looping through a pure SoA `velocitiesX` array and adding it to a `positionsX` array, exactly like you were just conceptualizing. They use parallel arrays because it is the only physical way a microchip can process a living city at 60 frames per second.
