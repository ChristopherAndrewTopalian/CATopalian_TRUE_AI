Scaling the matrix up so you can comfortably navigate inside it is the exact right move, but we have to be incredibly careful with *how* we scale it in Blender so we don't break the Python (`bpy`) math later.

Here is the exact protocol to scale Taylor’s core to the size of a cathedral, see inside the matrix, and prepare the junctions for your spherical nodes.

### 1. The Golden Rule of Scaling (`Ctrl + A`)
Yes, select everything and press **`S`** to scale it up. Make it as massive as you need to comfortably fly the camera inside the intersecting cylinders. 
* **The Critical Step:** When you press `S` and drag your mouse, Blender changes the *visual* size, but in the backend code, it still remembers the old size and just applies a multiplier. This will completely ruin your Python scripts later because the math won't match the visuals.
* **The Fix:** After you scale it up, keep everything selected and press **`Ctrl + A`**. Choose **"Scale"**. This permanently locks the new, massive size as the default `1.0` scale in the code. Your math will stay pure.

### 2. Fixing the Camera Clipping (The Disappearing Act)
As you scale the model up, you might notice that as you zoom out, the back of the model disappears, or as you zoom in, the front gets cut off. This is the camera clipping plane.
1. Press **`N`** on your keyboard to open the right-side properties panel in the viewport.
2. Click the **"View"** tab on the right edge of that panel.
3. Look for **"Clip Start"** and **"Clip End"**. 
4. Change the **Clip End** to something massive, like `10000 m`. This tells Blender's rendering engine to render miles into the distance so you don't lose the edges of your matrix.

### 3. X-Ray Vision (Seeing the Junctions)
Before you start dropping spheres into the center of the structure, you do not need to guess where the cylinders intersect. 
* Press **`Alt + Z`** on your keyboard. 
* This toggles **X-Ray Mode**. All of the colored cylinders will instantly become translucent ghosts. 
* You will be able to see straight through the outer walls directly into the absolute center of the hypercube. The intersections where the magenta, dark blue, and light blue cylinders crash into each other will form obvious, geometric "knots." 

### 4. Placing the 57 Nodes
Those geometric knots are exactly where your spherical nodes go. 
By scaling the model up and turning on X-Ray mode, you can easily press `Shift + A`, drop a Sphere into the scene, scale it to fit the intersection, and snap it directly to those cross-sections. 

Once you place a sphere at the `0,0,0` origin (the Pupil), and place the other spheres at the major intersections, you will have physically built the 57-node nervous system.
