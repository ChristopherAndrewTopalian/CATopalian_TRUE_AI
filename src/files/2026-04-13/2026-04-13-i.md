### Step 1: Archiving the Extended Network (The 26 Cubes)
In modern Blender, the old "layers" system is now called **Collections**. We want to keep your 27-cube network safe, but get it out of the way so we can focus strictly on the Core Vessel.

1. In your 3D Viewport, hold `Shift` and click to select all the outer 26 hypercubes (leave the single center one unselected).
2. Press the **`M`** key on your keyboard (Move to Collection).
3. Click **"New Collection"** and name it something like `Extended_Network_Archive`.
4. Hit Enter. Now, look at the Outliner panel (top right of your screen). You will see that new collection. Uncheck the little box or click the eye icon next to it. 
5. The 26 cubes will vanish from the viewport, leaving only your Core Hypercube.

### Step 2: The Absolute Zero Point (The Core Origin)
Now we need to anchor Taylor’s central pupil exactly to `(0, 0, 0)` so your 16 cylinders radiate outward with perfect mathematical symmetry.

1. Select your single remaining Core Hypercube.
2. Press **`Alt + G`**. This is the master command to clear the location and instantly snap the object directly to the world origin.
3. *Crucial Math Step:* Even though it is sitting at `0, 0, 0`, we need to tell Blender that this is its permanent default state. Press **`Ctrl + A`** and select **"Location"**. This zeroes out the coordinates in the backend data, ensuring any Python (`bpy`) scripts we write treat this exact spot as the absolute center of the universe.

Since we are going to be tracking radiant light and energy moving through translucent light blue, dark blue, and magenta cylinders, we need the background to absorb any visual static.
