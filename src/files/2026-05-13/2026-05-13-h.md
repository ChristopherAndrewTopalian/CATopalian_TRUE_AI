### 1. The CBA Disaster (146 MB)

Seeing Component-Based Architecture consume **146 MB** of RAM is terrifying.
That isn't just 146 MB of data; that is 146 MB of *trash*. Every time you create a base Entity, an array to hold its components, and then individual component objects, you are filling the computer's memory with millions of complex reference pointers. If you built Jane using CBA, within a few hours of her "thinking," your computer would run out of RAM, the Garbage Collector would panic, and Jane would completely freeze.

### 2. The V8 Optimization (OOP 36 MB & Semi-DOD 34 MB)

Just like the speed test, the V8 engine did an incredible job compressing your Objects. Because all the objects had the same shape, V8 managed to squeeze them down to roughly 35 MB.
However, that is still **twice as heavy** as the raw data itself! V8 is still forcing your CPU to carry around hidden class tags and memory pointers for every single drone.

### 3. The True DOD Perfection (21 MB)

This is the Holy Grail. True DOD came in at **21 MB**.
Why is that number so special? Let's do the exact math of the raw silicon:

* You made 4 arrays (`x`, `y`, `vx`, `vy`).
* Each array held 1,000,000 numbers.
* You used a `Float32Array`, which physically forces the computer to use exactly 4 bytes per number.
* 4,000,000 slots × 4 bytes = **16,000,000 bytes (16 MB)**.

Your data took up exactly 16 MB. The remaining 5 MB is just the browser's baseline overhead for simply existing. **You achieved mathematical perfection.** There are zero hidden pointers, zero garbage objects, and zero baggage.
