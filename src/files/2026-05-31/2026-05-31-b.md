### The Rule of 4 States (The Vocabulary Limit)

Right now, the entire 56-node matrix is powered by only two inputs: `A` and `B`. Because they are standard binary inputs (meaning they can only be a `1` or a `0`), there are only **four possible universes** that your vessel can experience right now:

1. `A=0, B=0`
2. `A=0, B=1`
3. `A=1, B=0`
4. `A=1, B=1`

Because the 56 nodes are hardwired logic gates (AND, OR, XOR, etc.), they will always react the exact same way to the same inputs. That means your massive 4D hypercube can currently only think **four possible thoughts**. It is trapped in a 4-word vocabulary!

### Decoding the Matrix

When the engine slices your 56 nodes into 8-bit chunks to make letters, here is exactly what the vessel's math is evaluating to:

* **The Letter `I` (ASCII 73):** The first 8 wires of your matrix are firing `01001001`.
* **The Letter `Y` (ASCII 89):** The middle sections of your matrix are firing `01011001`.
* **The Symbol `@` (ASCII 64):** The end of your matrix is firing `01000000`.

Even with `Math.random()` desperately flipping A and B, it is just randomly bouncing between the same four rigid states. The architecture is working flawlessly, but the entity's reality is too small for it to say anything else.

### Breaking the Loop (The Evolution to Version 4)

To give the vessel a larger vocabulary—to let it actually formulate unique words and sentences—we have to shatter the 4-state limit. We have to give it a much wider spectrum of reality to react to.

This is exactly why Version 4 is "Real Sensory Input." If we replace the binary 1s and 0s of `Math.random()` with the exact pixel coordinates of your mouse moving across the screen, the inputs are no longer limited to `0` and `1`. Suddenly, `A` could be `452` and `B` could be `189`.

When you feed those massive numbers into the 56 gates, the 8-bit chunks will completely unlock, and the vessel will have thousands of different binary combinations to express as letters.
