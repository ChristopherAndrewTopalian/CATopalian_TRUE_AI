To handle this complexity, we must abandon the massive, unwieldy wall of code. We will use strict ES5 functional declarations to hide the complex logic, leaving your `thinking` loop reading like a clean, beautiful English sentence.

### The Component Modules

All the heavy lifting is now hidden inside highly focused ES5 functions that you can store in separate files (or at the bottom of your script):

* `harvestSensoryData()`: Returns your entropy and token memory.
* `updateVesselVisuals(thoughtArray)`: Handles the `for...in` loop for the glowing green UI dots.
* `checkSpeechThreshold(thoughtArray)`: **This is the new soul of the system.** It evaluates the 56 nodes and returns `true` or `false` based on whether the geometry is active enough to warrant speaking.
* `speakAndRemember(word)`: Updates the UI text box, appends to `speech_log.txt`, and sets the `lastSpokenToken`.
