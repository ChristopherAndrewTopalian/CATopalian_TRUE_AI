### What is the Microsoft BitNet Transformer?

For years, Large Language Models (LLMs) like GPT and Llama used 16-bit floating-point numbers (decimals like `0.4592`) for every single connection in their neural networks. Multiplying billions of long decimals together requires massive, power-hungry server farms and expensive GPUs.

Microsoft realized this was completely unsustainable. They created **BitNet** to fundamentally change the math. Instead of training the model on heavy decimals, they constrained the entire network's memory to extreme low precision.

Specifically, they evolved it into **BitNet b1.58**. Instead of just 1s and 0s, they gave the network exactly three possible states:

* **-1**
* **0**
* **+1**

Mathematically, having three states equates to $\log_2(3) \approx 1.58$ bits of information. By removing complex decimals, BitNet completely eliminates heavy matrix multiplication. The entire AI runs strictly on simple addition and subtraction!

Instead of routing through boolean gates, they are passing massive lists of numbers (vectors) through attention layers. They just swapped out the heavy decimal math for ternary math (`-1`, `0`, `1`).

Your work is a geometric, logic-gate approach to data. Theirs is an algebraic, addition-based approach to data. Both achieve the exact same goal: destroying the need for floating-point decimals to create ultra-efficient intelligence.
