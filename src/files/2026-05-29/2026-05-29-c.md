Let's clear up the technical reality of how the machine reads your code, and then look at the human reality of how you have to build it.

### The Performance Reality (V8 Engine Physics)

To answer your biggest question directly: **No, the length of your function names has absolutely zero impact on execution speed or "thinking ability."** When a JavaScript engine (like V8) runs your code, it tokenizes and compiles everything down into machine code. To the processor, `N17()` and `node_017_tau_con_and_nor()` are both just memory addresses. They execute at the exact same lightning speed. The only place the character count matters is in the physical file size (kilobytes on the hard drive), which is completely negligible for what you are building.

You are not going to cause any lag with extra letters. The bottleneck in any matrix is the logic itself, not the names of the wires.

### Ambiguity vs. Memory

You nailed the Golden Rule of programming: **Avoid ambiguity at all costs.** When you are writing a pure functional library, dropping down to ultra-fast shorthand like `ge`, `ce`, or `ba` makes perfect sense for rapidly manipulating standard elements. It's clean and efficient. But you aren't just grabbing basic elements here—you are charting the literal dimensions of a 4D hypercube.

If you just use `N017()`, six months from now when you are debugging a logical short-circuit in the matrix, you will stare at `N017` and have absolutely no idea which wires are crossing there without digging through your documentation.

If you use `node_017_rp_lc_xnor_xor()`, the name of the function *is* the blueprint. It tells you exactly what horizontal and vertical planes are colliding at that exact coordinate. You won't have to memorize it, because the name tells you the physics of the node.

### The Final Taxonomy

Your instincts are spot-on. Here is why your final choice of `node_017_...` is the most professional and scalable way to structure this:

* **The Primitives:** Leaving the base 16 genesis gates as simple, short names (`tau`, `con`, `and`, `lp`) beautifully separates them from the structural intersections. They are the prime elements.
* **The Prefix (`node_`):** This explicitly tells the system (and you) that this function represents a physical structural intersection, not a prime element.
* **The Chronology (`017_`):** Padding the number with a zero keeps your files and arrays perfectly sorted in your directory. `node_017` will neatly sit right above `node_018` instead of letting the OS jumble `1`, `10`, `11`, `2`.
* **The Suffix (`_rp_lc_xnor_xor`):** This is the wiring diagram.

By adopting `node_017_...`, you get the mathematical sorting of numbers *and* the precise structural mapping of words.
