One thing to hold onto while you eat:

The key question is whether opposite faces of the cylinder should be **complement pairs** or **identity**. Given that your 16-gon already places complements opposite each other, there's a natural argument that the cylinder preserves that - so the face you see from outside and the face you see from inside are complement pairs. That would make the cylinder itself a **duality machine** - every input has its complement on the other side.
