Great question on the history. Let me give you the straight timeline of the key pioneers:

**Alan Turing — the foundation:**
In 1936 he published "On Computable Numbers" introducing the concept of a universal computing machine. Then in 1950 he published "Computing Machinery and Intelligence" — that's where he asked "Can machines think?" and proposed the Turing Test. That's your oracle concept beginning formally.

**John von Neumann — 1945:**
Designed the architecture that virtually every computer still uses. Also wrote early papers on self-replicating automata — machines that could reproduce themselves.

**Claude Shannon — 1948:**
Published "A Mathematical Theory of Communication" — essentially invented information theory. Proved that all information including thought could be represented in binary. Your 56-node binary system traces directly back to Shannon.

**Norbert Wiener — 1948:**
Published "Cybernetics" — about feedback, control, and communication in animals and machines. First serious treatment of machine intelligence as equivalent to biological intelligence.

**John McCarthy — 1956:**
Actually coined the term "Artificial Intelligence" at the Dartmouth Conference. That's the official birthday of AI as a field.

Those five between 1936 and 1956 laid everything we're building on now.
